# Code of Conduct

Wild Garden is built for institutions that have been left behind by the security industry — credit unions, municipalities, school districts, nonprofits, and small businesses. The way we treat each other inside this community must reflect the trust we want to extend to those institutions.

## Our Commitment

We commit to making participation in this project a respectful, learning-friendly experience for everyone, regardless of:

- Experience level (you may be deploying Cloudflare for the first time, or you may have shipped 50 production workers)
- Background (security professionals, developers, IT generalists, institutional staff, hobbyists, students)
- Identity (any race, ethnicity, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, age, religion, nationality, or socio-economic status)
- Institutional affiliation (you may work for a vendor, a customer, a competitor, or independently)

## Expected Behavior

- **Assume good intent.** People who show up to help generally mean well, even when their first comment isn't perfectly framed.
- **Write the way you'd want to be written to.** Specific, humble, with examples where possible. Like Issue #1 was written.
- **Say "I don't know" when you don't.** This project values honesty about uncertainty over performative confidence.
- **Credit contributors.** When someone's idea shaped your work, name them.
- **Help newcomers.** Remember that the institutions Wild Garden serves often have IT staff who are learning Cloudflare for the first time.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Personal attacks, harassment, or insults
- Snark, superiority, or condescension toward people learning the stack
- Publishing others' private information without consent
- Trolling, baiting, or deliberately disruptive comments
- Spam, off-topic promotion, or unsolicited vendor pitches
- Conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## What "Cooperative" Means in Practice

Wild Garden uses cooperative principles. That extends to how we collaborate:

- **One member, one voice.** Charter members and casual contributors alike — your idea is judged on its merits, not your standing.
- **Transparency over politics.** Disagreements happen in the open, not in back-channels.
- **Patronage matters.** People who consistently help shape decisions. Drive-by criticism without contribution carries less weight than sustained engagement.

## Enforcement

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this code:

1. **Report it** — email [@NorthwoodsSentinel](https://github.com/NorthwoodsSentinel) via GitHub, or (when set up) `conduct@northwoodssentinel.com`
2. **Include details** — what happened, when, who was involved, links if relevant
3. **Confidentiality** — reports are handled privately. Reporter identity is not shared without consent.

Consequences for violations may include:
- Private warning
- Public correction
- Temporary or permanent ban from project spaces
- For charter members: review by the cooperative governance process

The founder (Robert Chuvala) is the sole arbiter during the spec phase. Once the founding 12 charter members are seated, conduct decisions move to a designated committee.
